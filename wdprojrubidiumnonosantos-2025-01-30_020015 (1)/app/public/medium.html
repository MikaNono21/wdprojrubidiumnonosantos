Case File 2: The Disappearance of the Little Prince

Evidences at the Crime Scene

1. Torn Cloak: A piece of fabric, identified as part of the prince’s cloak, was found near the castle wall. The fabric shows signs of struggle, indicating that the prince might have been forcibly taken or tried to escape.

2. Footprints: A trail of footprints was discovered leading away from the castle grounds toward the nearby woods. The prints appear to be fresh and suggest that the prince was moved quickly.

3. Witness Testimonies:

  1. • Servant: A servant reported seeing the young prince talking to someone near the castle garden just before his disappearance. The servant couldn’t identify the person but mentioned seeing a shadowy figure approaching the prince.
  2. • Guard: A castle guard mentioned hearing a struggle near the castle wall but didn’t see anyone. The guard has been stationed in a different section of the castle and didn’t get a good look at the suspect.
  3. • Townsperson: A townsfolk nearby claimed to have seen a figure carrying something wrapped in a cloth around the time of the disappearance. They mentioned that the person was heading toward the old part of the town, but the figure’s identity was unclear

Suspects

Hover to see evidence for each suspect. Choose wisely, Detective!

The Royal Butler: Known for his close relationship with the royal family, the butler had access to the prince at all times. He was seen near the castle gardens on the day of the disappearance. The torn piece of the cloak could have come from the prince while struggling to break free from the butler’s grasp.
The Knight: A member of the royal guard, the knight had a recent argument with the prince about his behavior. He was seen leaving the castle early in the evening and was nowhere to be found when the prince went missing. Could the prince’s rebellious nature have caused him to take drastic action?
The Town Merchant: A merchant recently in conflict with the prince over unpaid debts. Seen lingering near the castle on the day of the disappearance, their strange behavior suggests they might have taken the prince to send a message about their financial troubles.
The Princess: The young prince’s older sister, often protective but recently seen arguing with the prince over family matters. While unlikely, she was the last person known to be in close proximity to the prince before he vanished. Did sibling rivalry play a role in the disappearance?
