Case File 1: The Robbery of a Family Heirloom

Hello, Detective, and welcome to your first case! The royal family admires your skills and is counting on you. We believe you have what it takes to solve this mystery—failure is not an option. Prove us right, and a generous cash prize awaits. Good luck!

Evidences at the Crime Scene

1. Suspicious Footprints: A set of footprints was discovered near the castle basement door. The prints appear fresh, leading away from the entrance and toward a side exit of the castle. The footprints are too small to belong to a fully grown adult, raising questions about the thief’s identity.

2. Basement Door Marks: There are subtle signs of forced entry around the basement door lock, suggesting the thief had to break in. However, there are no signs of struggle or alarms triggered, meaning the thief was likely familiar with the castle and its security systems.

3. Witness Testimonies:

  1. • The Castle Guard: The guard on duty reported seeing a figure near the basement around the time of the theft. However, the figure was wearing a cloak, making it difficult to identify. The guard mentioned hearing faint footsteps but did not stop the figure, assuming it was a castle worker.
  2. • The Housekeeper: The housekeeper stated she had seen the necklace in the basement the day before, but she did not notice anything unusual until the theft was discovered. She mentioned that only a few trusted individuals knew about the heirloom’s location.
  3. • The Lady-in-Waiting: The Lady-in-Waiting, who is often in and out of the basement for various errands, seemed unusually nervous when questioned. She claimed to have no knowledge of the necklace’s disappearance but appeared agitated when asked about her whereabouts during the time of the theft.

Suspects

Hover to see evidence for each suspect. Choose wisely, Detective!

The Castle Guard: Assigned near the basement, the guard was aware of all activities. His vague description of a cloaked figure and expertise in security suggest he might have stolen the necklace without triggering alarms.
The Lady-in-Waiting: As a trusted member of the royal staff, the Lady-in-Waiting had frequent access to the basement. Her nervous behavior and unusual agitation when questioned make her a prime suspect. It’s possible that she knew the necklace was there and took it for herself, hoping no one would suspect her.
The Housekeeper: The housekeeper is one of the few people who knew about the necklace’s location. She was responsible for cleaning the basement and might have had the opportunity to take the heirloom while in the area. However, her calm demeanor when questioned raises doubts about her involvement.
