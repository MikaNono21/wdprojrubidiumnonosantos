Case File 3: The Murder of the Loyal Gardener

Evidences at the Crime Scene

1. Blood-Stained Gardening Tool: A gardening tool, likely the weapon used in the murder, was found next to the body. It is covered in blood, with traces of the gardener’s skin found under the handle, indicating a violent confrontation. The tool appears to have been dropped hastily by the killer.

2. Broken Shards of Glass: Nearby, the detective found broken glass scattered across the ground. It appears to be from a shattered vase or window, though it’s unclear if the glass is related to the murder or if it was part of a struggle. Some shards have bloodstains, suggesting they might have been involved in the altercation.

3. Strange Footprints: Unusual footprints lead away from the body, headed toward the estate’s back gate. The prints are of a large boot, possibly worn by a man, and appear to have been made in haste, as if the person was fleeing the scene. The prints lead into the nearby woods, but the trail is difficult to follow due to the terrain.

4. Gardener’s Personal Effects: The gardener’s pocket watch, which is typically worn around his neck, was found in the garden, broken open. There are signs of a struggle nearby, and it is possible that the gardener tried to defend himself before the attack.

5. Witness Testimonies:

  1. • The Royal Family’s Maid: The maid was one of the last to see the gardener alive. She stated that she saw him speaking to someone outside the royal garden gate earlier that day but couldn’t see the other person clearly. She mentioned the gardener seemed uneasy but didn’t indicate any trouble.

  2. • The Royal Butler: The butler, who had a routine of overseeing the royal garden, mentioned hearing a loud noise coming from the yard in the late afternoon. He rushed outside but arrived too late to stop the crime. The butler did not see the gardener’s attacker but suspected someone had entered the garden through the back gate.

  3. • The Neighboring Farmer: A neighboring farmer, who lives just outside the royal estate, heard shouting around the time of the murder. He said that he saw a figure fleeing from the scene but could not make out any details because it was getting dark. He did mention that the figure appeared to be carrying something, though he couldn’t confirm if it was the gardening tool or something else.

Suspects

Hover to see evidence for each suspect. Choose wisely, Detective!

The Royal Butler: Known for his close relationship with the royal family, the butler had access to the prince at all times. He was seen near the castle gardens on the day of the disappearance. The torn piece of the cloak could have come from the prince while struggling to break free from the butler’s grasp.
The Neighboring Farmer: The farmer’s presence near the estate and report of hearing shouting raise suspicion. He may have known the gardener’s routine or held a grudge against the royal family, and his claim of seeing a fleeing figure suggests deeper involvement.
The Royal Family’s Maid: Although the maid seemed to have a neutral relationship with the gardener, her nervousness when questioned raises suspicion. She may have known something about the gardener’s personal life or been involved in a hidden dispute, though her motive seems weaker than the other suspects.
The Gardener’s Close Friend (The Apprentice): The apprentice had a personal relationship with the gardener, and rumors of a recent falling out raise suspicion. Though not seen at the scene, jealousy or resentment could have driven the attack, and the gardener’s absence may indicate tension between them.
